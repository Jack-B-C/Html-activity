Treaty of Waitangi

The Treaty of Waitangi (or Te Tiriti o Waitangi in Māori), is New Zealand's founding document written in 1840.
It is a document stating the agreement between the British Crown and the Māori to live in unity.

The Signing of the Treaty of Waitangi

The British government intended to annex New Zealand in attempt to regulate the growing number of British migrants moving to New Zealand, secure commercial interests, and protect the Māori.
The Māori were convinced to sign the Treaty, unaware the effects British sovereignty will have due to a translation issue between the English and Māori copies.
After over 500 Māori chiefs signed the treaty, British sovereignty over New Zealand was proclaimed on May 21st, 1840.
